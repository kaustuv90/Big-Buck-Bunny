				<h1>Download Guide: Big Buck Bunny, Sunflower version</h1>
				<p>The movie is available in several different sizes and formats. To get the best 
				experience you should pick the download that suits your hardware. We recommended 
				trying out the 60 fps version and that you switch to the 30 fps version if your
				hardware has issues decoding the video.</p>
				<p>When viewing the video you should pick a resolution close to the native resolution of your screen or
				projector, then view the movie at "1-to-1" or "100% scale" - avoid scaling the video up or down.</p>
				<p>All of the videos are encoded with <a href="http://www.videolan.org/developers/x264.html">x264</a> and contain both an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolby_AC-3">AC-3</a> 5.1 surround sound track as well as a normal
				<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MP3">mp3</a> stereo track. It should play fine in players like <a href="http://www.videolan.org/vlc/">VLC</a> or <a href="http://sourceforge.net/projects/mpc-hc/files/MPC%20HomeCinema%20-%20Win32/MPC-HC_v1.7.1_x86/">MPC-HC</a> (for 2D) and players such as <a href="http://bino3d.org/">Bino3D</a> or the <a href="http://www.3dtv.at/Downloads/Index_en.aspx">3Dtv stereoscopic player</a> (for 3D).

				<p>The videos come in three different categories:
				<ol>
					<li>
						Hardware-assisted stereoscopic 3D:<br />
						You will need dedicated software and hardware to view this kind of movie. Typically
						this hardware includes a 3D-enabled screen or projector, a pair of 
						glasses and something to decode the special video format. Each image in
						the video is encoded as two eyes, <b>one above the other, left eye on top</b>.
					</li>
					<li>
						Colour-coded stereoscopic 3D, known as anaglyph 3D:<br />
						This kind of movie can be played back on normal 2D-capable hardware but
						requires a special pair of glasses with a different kind of tint for each
						eye. The 3D effect is not as nice as with the real 3D movies, but the setup
						is a lot cheaper and easier to get started. Each one is named after the <b>colours
						of the glass, left eye first</b>.
					</li>
					<li>
						And finally the 2D version:<br />
						If you do not have 3D stereo equipment you will still be able to enjoy the original
						2D movie in higher framerate and higher resolution.
						For the 4K videos you will most likely need a very powerfull CPU with either a multicore
						software decoder (ffmpeg, lavf, CoreAVC) or GPU hardware decoding support like PureVideo 5 
						on the Nvidia GTX 600- and 700-series or OpenVideo Decode support on AMD cards.

					</li>
				</ol>

				or the magnet link. If you cannot get BitTorrent to work try out the HTTP links.</p>
